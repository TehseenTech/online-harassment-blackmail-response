# Online Harassment, Blackmail & Impersonation Response

> Educational, safety-first guidance for people responding to cyber incidents. This project is independent and is not a law-enforcement agency, law firm, bank, telecom operator, or account-recovery service.

Safety-first guidance for documenting and reporting cyber harassment, blackmail, threats, stalking, intimate-image abuse, and impersonation.

## If the incident is happening now

- If there is immediate physical danger, contact local emergency services and move to a safer location.
- Do not meet, pay, or negotiate with a blackmailer without advice from a qualified authority or lawyer.
- Preserve messages, usernames, profile URLs, phone numbers, payment demands, threats, and timestamps before blocking or reporting.
- Tighten account privacy, remove unknown sessions, enable multi-factor authentication, and limit public location information.
- Report the account and content to the platform, then report qualifying cybercrime to NCCIA.
- Tell a trusted person; avoid handling threats alone.

## Evidence checklist

- Full conversation exports where the platform supports them
- Screenshots showing account name, handle, URL, date, and surrounding context
- Original images, videos, audio, emails, and voice notes without editing
- A log of every report, response, case number, and person who handled a copy

## What not to do

- Do not publicly repost intimate content or threats as 'proof'.
- Do not crop away usernames, timestamps, or context from the only copy.
- Do not threaten retaliation, attempt unauthorized access, or impersonate the offender.

## Simple incident timeline

| Date and time | Time zone | Event | Account or URL | Evidence file | Action taken | Reference number |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM | PKT / UTC+5 | What happened | Identifier | File name | Report or containment step | Ticket / complaint ID |

Keep facts separate from assumptions. Record times exactly as displayed and note the time zone. Preserve original files; use copies for review or redaction.

## Reporting in Pakistan

For cybercrime complaints, use the official [NCCIA complaint portal](https://complaint.nccia.gov.pk/) or the current reporting methods listed on the [NCCIA website](https://www.nccia.gov.pk/). For immediate physical danger, contact local emergency services. For urgent financial loss, contact the relevant bank, wallet, card issuer, or payment provider first.

## Privacy and repository safety

This is a public repository. **Do not open an issue containing victim names, CNIC or passport numbers, phone numbers, financial details, credentials, private media, chat exports, case files, or other evidence.** Use official private reporting channels. Redact examples before sharing them for documentation improvements.
